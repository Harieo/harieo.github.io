How do I work?

I take on projects based on multiple factors which include, but aren't limited to, my availability, whether the project is suitable for my skill set and the scale of the project.

Availability depends on any projects I've taken on before yours and any other obligations I may have in my personal life. I won't take on a project that I can't complete and will inform clients of any delays that may occur during coding.

Suitability depends on whether your project will require any mechanics or techniques that I'm not capable of providing you. I don't know everything and will do my best to ensure your project is completed to an acceptable standard. If I can't fulfil this, I will reject the project outright.

Scale depends on the sheer amount of work your project would require and the resources it will take to complete. This is something I'd personally discuss with a client to get a clear understanding of the complexity and scale of the project, which would allow me to assess whether I will be able to dedicate the resources to completing it. This also ties in with my availability, the longer the project will take, the more available I will need to make myself.

Once a project is accepted, we will come up with a design and requirements for the project to make sure it will be completed how you would like it. After all this, the coding can finally begin and I will provide demonstrations on the progress of the project at stages where appropriate. More details will be given upon a project being accepted.
